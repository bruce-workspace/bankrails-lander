The Problem

Your agents are ready to work.
Your payment infrastructure isn't.

Right now, the only options for giving an AI agent access to funds are blunt instruments built for humans, not agents.

option_a

Hand it your credit card

No controls. No limits. No audit trail. Your agent has the same access as your CEO — and zero accountability for how it spends.

  • No per-agent spending limits
  • No transaction conditions or logic
  • No attribution or compliance trail
option_b

Give it a prepaid card

A balance and a limit — that's it. There's no way to tell it: only spend on approved vendors, require sign-off over $100, or stop if the transaction looks unusual.

  • No conditional logic or approval rules
  • No vendor whitelisting or blocking
  • No compliance reporting
option_c

Give it nothing

Your autonomous agent pipeline stalls every time it hits a payment. You're back in the loop, manually approving every transaction. That's not agentic — that's just automation with extra steps.

  • Human bottleneck on every transaction
  • Blocks multi-agent workflows entirely
  • Zero progress toward actual autonomy
the_gap

Neither was built for agents. Both create real exposure. There's no infrastructure today that lets you set different budgets for different agents in the same workflow — with conditional logic, compliance reporting, and real settlement.

That's what BankRails is.

The Solution

Programmable payment logic
built for the agent era.

BankRails gives each AI agent a verified payment identity with the controls that actually matter.

per_agent_budgets

Per-agent budgets and limits

Set spending limits by agent, by category, by time window. Your orchestrator agent can have a $10K project budget. Your sub-agents get $200 each. Different agents, different controls — all in one workflow.

agent: "orchestrator_v2"
budget: $10,000 // project
sub_agents: $200 // each
conditional_logic

Conditional transaction logic

Money moves when conditions are met — not before. Define approval rules, spending triggers, and automatic holds based on transaction type, amount, or agent behavior.

audit_trail

Full audit trail

Every transaction logged, timestamped, and attributed to a specific agent. Built for compliance from the ground up. When your CFO asks, you have the answer.

banking_rails

Real banking rails

Transactions settle through real banking infrastructure. No crypto. No stablecoins. Chargebacks, dispute resolution, and counterparty protection that enterprise finance teams actually require.

KYA

Know Your Agent

Verify the agents transacting in your system. Whitelist trusted agents, block unknown ones, and get alerts when something looks wrong.

Agent-to-agent payments

Pay other agents directly. The foundation for agent commerce: your orchestrator allocates budgets, sub-agents transact independently, everything settles cleanly.

Why Not Crypto?

It works in boardrooms,
not just Discord.

Skyfire and others settle agent transactions in USDC on the blockchain. That works fine for developers and crypto-native startups.

It doesn't work for the companies actually deploying AI at scale:

no_chargebacks

Once it's gone, it's gone. No dispute resolution, no recourse on bad transactions.

treasury_exposure

Stablecoin treasury exposure on every transaction. Not a conversation any CFO wants to have.

procurement_blocked

Hard no from any mid-market or enterprise CFO. Regulatory uncertainty makes procurement conversations impossible.

regulatory_risk

The rules are still being written. That's not a foundation you want under your payment infrastructure.
