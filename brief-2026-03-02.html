Competitive Positioning for BankRails

Own the regulated wedge. x402 + ERC-8004 is the decentralized agent payment stack. BankRails needs a clear answer for how it interoperates with x402 — or, better, positions itself as the compliant gateway x402 can't be for insurance carriers, factoring companies, and regulated B2B flows.

Visa and Mastercard are moving enterprise-slow. Their Agent Pay and Intelligent Commerce initiatives are real but deliberate. The window for a nimble rail to lock in insurance and factoring workflows (BankRails' natural wedge) is open now — it won't be indefinitely.

Scale expectations are already set high. 120M transactions/week from one consumer deployment. B2B flows are lower volume but demand higher compliance fidelity. That's the differentiation: regulated-industry reliability the crypto-native stack can't deliver.

Guardrails + audit trails = the enterprise narrative. MoonPay, Wooshpay, and x402 all lead with agent autonomy. BankRails can own "trusted agent payment rail with spend controls, audit logs, and regulatory compliance" — the thing enterprises actually need before they let an AI agent move money.
